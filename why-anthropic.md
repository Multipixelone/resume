I've been following neural networks since people were training them to beat old NES games. Drawn to the field, and clear-eyed about how much these systems can do. At their core LLMs are just very fancy text-prediction engines, but give them enough scale and they turn into something far stranger and more capable than that makes them sound. That power cuts both ways: the same scale that makes LLMs useful makes them easy to misuse, and I think that risk is incredibly real. That's the part of Anthropic's position that actually makes me want to work with you guys:building capable models while treating safety as a genuine research problem.
